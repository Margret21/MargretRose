Flying Dog Animal Rescue League

Branding

I designed the logo for Flying Dogs Animal Rescue League as a light, no-nonsense design. This logo will be showed to clients who will hopefully be donating to this business. Those people will most likely be middle aged, middle to upper class and if they want to donate money they will be looking for a well established, trustworthy company. To make my logo show that I made it simple. I chose to only use black and not add a lot of imagery. I chose to use the font Nella Sue Doma because I felt like it complimented the style of the dog outline. It has the same almost sketchy feel, but it’s clean and readable.

The business card I designed with a few other colors including grey and blue. I used these colors because they aren’t very distracting and they still follow that trustworthy feel. Anything dealing with money makes that trust really important and my main goal was to convey that. I tried to keep the card simple, so it didn’t become too confusing or overwhelming and I made sure that the dog in the logo was easy to see and understand. I really wanted to make sure that people could see the dog and know what the company was about and also that Nancy’s name was large and easy to read.

  • Date: November 2015
  • Client: Flying Dog Animal Rescue League
  • Category: Branding Package

Art Nouveau Self Portrait

Illustration

The prompt for this project was to create a self promotional poster modeled after a concert poster. I decided to focus on the art nouveau style because it’s one of my favorites and as a French major I felt that it fit my personality well. I then added a crown of pen tool symbols to represent my designer side. I vectorized the whole poster including the image of me using Illustrator then put all of the elements together in InDesign.

  • Date: April 2014
  • Client: Class Project
  • Category: Illustrated Poster
